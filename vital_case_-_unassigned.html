Buzz Words and Phrases (ABCS guide)

expand_less

At times in addition to highly out of range vitals, or symptomatic conditions described by a patient, certain buzz words can help determine the criticality of patient’s condition that may necessitate escalation.


Below is a suggested list of key words to look out for when engaging with patients to gather their symptoms and if encountered, please be prepared to directly intimate the provider (by phone or text) in addition to clearly highlighting it on your escalation notes.


A

Airway (Breathing)

• "Choking" / "Gagging"

• "Can't catch my breath"

• "Difficulty Breathing

• "Hard time breathing"

• "Feel out of breath"

• Patient having a hard time speaking in full sentences

• Abnormal Breathing heard over the phone

• On oxygen at home


B

Bleeding

• Ask bleeding patient about blood thinner use. (Any amount of bleeding)

• "Blood pouring out"

• "Won't stop bleeding"

• "Lots of blood"

• "Large amount of bleeding."

• Patient reports blood in vomit

• Patient reports moderate to large amount of bleeding from rectum


C

Chest Pain

• "Chest pain"

• "Chest pressure"

• "Heavy feeling in my chest"

• "Weight on chest"

• "Feels like I was punched in the chest"

• "Burning in chest"

• "Irregular heart beat"

• "Heart beat too fast"

• Chest pain with: sudden pain in arm, neck, or jaw, cold sweat, nausea or vomiting.


S

Stroke or Suicidal

• Sudden numbness or weakness one side of body, (face, arm, leg)

• Sudden slurred speech

• Sudden drooping of face to one side

• Sudden severe headache

• Sudden onset of confusion

• Sudden loss of coord, balance, and onset of dizziness

• "I should kill myself"

• "I want to die"

• "I'm tired of living"
